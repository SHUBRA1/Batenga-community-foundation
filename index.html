Who We Are

A Foundation Built on Faith and Community

Batenga Community Foundation has been walking alongside Uganda's most vulnerable since 2015. We focus on widows and orphans, providing not just material support but a path to lasting dignity through housing, education, and skills training.

We operate from Nansana East II, Nansana Town Council, Wakiso District, with an additional office in Kampala, and our work spans Kyankwanzi and Buikwe Districts.

Duly registered with the Government of Uganda and incorporated as a Company Limited by Guarantee with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) under Registration No. 80034651950097.

Vision

A world where every citizen possesses the knowledge, skills, and civic competencies to contribute to the creation and maintenance of balanced and compatible societies.

Mission

Batenga Foundation is dedicated to equipping citizens with tools, knowledge, and methodologies that enhance civic competence and promote best practices at individual, group, and governmental levels.

What We Do

Programs Rooted in Real Need

Every initiative we run addresses a specific gap felt by vulnerable families in Uganda. We do not work from a distance.

Housing Project

We have constructed 40 homes for widows and vulnerable families: 21 in Kyankwanzi District and 19 in Buikwe District. Each home is valued at UGX 5 million, totaling approximately USD 60,000 invested in shelter and safety.

Orphan Sponsorship

Education support has been extended to over 40 orphaned children across primary and secondary schools in Uganda, giving them a genuine shot at a self-reliant future.

Health Camps

We run community health camps to bring medical attention directly to rural and underserved populations, reducing the distance between people and basic healthcare.

Water and Sanitation

We provide boreholes and solar-managed water sources to communities facing water scarcity, ensuring access to clean and safe water for daily use.

Food Relief

We deliver food packages to expecting mothers, the elderly, and disadvantaged persons. Packages include rice, sugar, beans, maize flour, wheat flour, and milk.

Community Outreach

Beyond our core projects, we run outreach programs that bring skills, civic knowledge, and community support directly to people in their own neighborhoods.
